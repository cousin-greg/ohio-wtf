# Ohayo Lead Generation Playbook

## Target Verticals (from strategy.md)
1. Real estate agents & small brokerages
2. Health, wellness & fitness studios
3. Creative freelancers & remote solopreneurs
4. Restaurants & food businesses
5. Property management companies
6. Local service businesses (HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, cleaning)
7. Law firms & accountants (small practices)
8. Dental / medical offices (small)
9. Auto shops & detailing
10. Any small business with visible manual-process pain

## Geography
- Primary: Boulder, CO
- Secondary: Longmont, Louisville, Lafayette, Broomfield, Erie
- Tertiary: Denver metro (if boulder list is thin)

## What Makes a Good Lead
- Small business (1-50 employees)
- Has a website (means some tech awareness)
- Visible signs of manual processes (no online booking, contact form only, "call to schedule", outdated website, no CRM evident)
- Active on social media but inconsistent (means they want to but can't keep up)
- Hiring for admin/ops roles (means they're drowning)
- Reviews mentioning slow response times (automation opportunity)

## Lead Scoring (0-100)
- Has website: +10
- Website looks outdated or basic: +15 (more likely to need help)
- No online booking/scheduling: +15
- "Call to schedule" or "email us": +10
- Hiring admin/ops roles: +20
- Social media present but inconsistent: +10
- Google reviews mention slow communication: +15
- Multiple locations or growing: +10
- Industry match (real estate, wellness, restaurants): +10

## Output Format
Each lead gets:
- Business name
- Category/vertical
- Address
- Phone
- Website
- Contact name (if findable)
- Email (if findable)
- Score (0-100)
- Pain signals (what we spotted)
- Suggested pitch angle
- Source (where we found them)
