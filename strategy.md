## Target Customer Profiles

### 1. Real Estate Agents & Small Brokerages

**Why they're perfect:**
- Boulder County has 4,400+ active agents — massive addressable market
- Commission-based income means they *feel* time waste directly in their wallet
- Most are solopreneurs or small teams with no tech staff
- Repetitive workflows everywhere: lead follow-up, listing updates, market reports, client communication, showing scheduling

**Pain points Greg solves:**
- Automated lead nurturing sequences (new inquiry → personalized follow-up → drip campaign)
- Market report generation (pull comps, format, send — automated weekly)
- Client communication bots (Telegram/SMS updates on listing status)
- CRM data entry automation
- Social media content scheduling from listings

**Willingness to pay:** High. A single saved deal from better follow-up pays for a year of Greg's services. Agents in Boulder's market (median home ~$800K+) have real money to spend.

### 2. Health, Wellness & Fitness Studios

**Why they're perfect:**
- Boulder is the wellness capital of Colorado — yoga studios, CrossFit boxes, climbing gyms, functional medicine, massage therapists, nutritionists everywhere
- Most are owner-operated with 1-3 employees
- Drowning in admin: scheduling, follow-ups, class reminders, billing, social media
- Tech-curious but time-poor

**Pain points Greg solves:**
- Automated class reminders and waitlist management
- New client onboarding sequences
- Review request automation (post-visit → "Leave us a review?" text)
- Social content scheduling
- Membership renewal reminders and win-back campaigns

**Willingness to pay:** Medium-high. These businesses run on thin margins but understand that losing clients to poor follow-up is expensive. $500-1,500/month is within reach for a studio doing $15K+/month revenue.

### 3. Creative Freelancers & Remote Solopreneurs

**Why they're perfect:**
- Boulder is packed with remote workers, freelance designers, writers, developers, photographers, coaches
- They understand tech and won't need hand-holding on basics
- Their pain is admin/operations eating into billable hours
- Many already use some tools but nothing is connected

**Pain points Greg solves:**
- Invoice and payment follow-up automation
- Client onboarding workflows
- Project status dashboards
- Email triage and prioritization

**Willingness to pay:** Medium. More price-sensitive individually, but high volume. Also the best word-of-mouth network in Boulder — one freelancer tells their coworking space, and suddenly you have 10 leads.

## Pricing Strategy

**Structure: Hybrid (project-based entry → retainer conversion)**

The play is simple:
1. **Workshops** generate awareness and leads (free or $1,500)
2. **Jumpstarts** ($500) convert curious leads into paying clients with minimal risk
3. **Buildouts** ($1,500-3,500) deliver big value for clients who want specific systems
4. **Retainers** ($2,000/month) are the real business — recurring, predictable, scalable

**Why not hourly?** Hourly punishes efficiency. If Greg builds in 2 hours what someone else takes 10, he shouldn't earn less. Project and retainer pricing rewards results, not time.

**Boulder pricing context:** Boulder's median household income is ~$80K, but the business owners and freelancers Greg targets earn significantly more. $500 for a Jumpstart is less than a nice dinner for two on Pearl Street. $2K/month retainer is a rounding error for a real estate agent closing $800K homes.

## Risks & Challenges

### 1. "AI" Fatigue
**Risk:** People are tired of hearing about AI. Everyone's pitching it.
**Mitigation:** Greg doesn't sell "AI" — he sells time savings. Lead with outcomes ("I'll save you 10 hours a week"), not technology ("I'll implement AI automations"). Show, don't tell.

### 2. Client Dependency
**Risk:** Clients can't maintain automations without Greg, leading to support burden.
**Mitigation:** Document everything. Build simple systems, not clever ones. The Buildout package includes a walkthrough for a reason. For retainer clients, this *is* the business model — they pay for ongoing support.

### 3. Scope Creep
**Risk:** "Can you also just quickly..." is the death of profitability.
**Mitigation:** Crystal-clear scoping on Buildouts. Retainers have hour caps. Anything beyond scope gets quoted as a new project. Be friendly about it, but firm.

### 4. Market Education
**Risk:** Potential clients don't know what's possible, so they don't know to look for Greg.
**Mitigation:** Workshops solve this. So does content (short videos showing real automations). The Jumpstart's low price point also helps — $500 to find out what's possible is easy to say yes to.

### 5. Platform Risk
**Risk:** Tools Greg builds on (APIs, platforms) change or break.
**Mitigation:** Build on stable foundations. Diversify tool stack. Retainer model inherently includes maintenance. Be transparent with clients about this reality — it's actually a selling point for ongoing retainers.

### 6. Scaling Ceiling
**Risk:** Greg is one person. 10 retainer clients = 100 hours/month of committed time, plus everything else.
**Mitigation:** Be deliberate about growth. At 10+ retainers, either raise prices, get selective, or bring on a subcontractor. Don't chase volume at the expense of quality — reputation is everything in a town like Boulder.
